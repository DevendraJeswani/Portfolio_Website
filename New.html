Reflections

What I've Learned

Real lessons from internships, competitions, and self-initiated work. Not from textbooks.

Society greatly rewards guts From my experience, taking bold decisions has been one of my biggest strengths, while hesitation at key moments has sometimes cost me valuable opportunities.
Shipping beats planning A good solution shipped today outperforms a perfect one shipped next month. Bias for action isn't recklessness - it's prioritizing learning over certainty.
Data without context is noise A 40% drop-off means nothing without knowing where users came from, what they expected, and what they found instead. Context converts data into decisions.
Alignment is the actual product You can build the right thing and still fail if you haven't brought stakeholders along. Communication isn't soft skill - it's the core of PM work.
Constraints drive better decisions The best choices I've made came when time or resources were limited. Constraints force prioritization - which is the heart of what PMs actually do.
Make assumptions explicit Every strategy has assumptions baked in. Most teams just don't name them. Naming them, ranking by risk, and testing the riskiest first is how you avoid expensive surprises.
